üí∞Move 2: Build a No-Stress Budget

Do not save what is left after spending, but spend what is left after saving.

‚Äî Warren Buffett

Forget complicated spreadsheets. The 50/30/15/5 rule is the simplest system for couples who want to build wealth without constant money stress.

üìä The 50/30/15/5 Breakdown

Category Percentage Example ($5,000/month)
Fixed Costs (rent, utilities, groceries) 50% $2,500
Lifestyle (dining, entertainment, fun) 30% $1,500
Investments (retirement, index funds) 15% $750
Savings Goals (house, vacation, emergency) 5% $250

This system works because it's guilt-free. You both know exactly how much you can spend on lifestyle without touching your future. No more "Should we really buy this?" conversations.

‚Äî Ramit Sethi

üí° Action Step #2

‚úÇÔ∏èMove 4: Eliminate Toxic Debt Together

The borrower is slave to the lender.

‚Äî Benjamin Franklin

Not all debt is created equal. Your mortgage? That's building wealth. Your credit cards at 24% interest? That's wealth destruction on steroids.

The Debt Avalanche Method: List all debts by interest rate (highest first). Pay minimums on everything, then throw every extra dollar at the highest rate debt. It's mathematically optimal and psychologically powerful when you tackle it as a team.

üèîÔ∏è Debt Avalanche in Action

Credit Card: $8,000 at 22% interest
Car Loan: $15,000 at 6% interest
Student Loan: $25,000 at 4% interest

Strategy: Attack the credit card first while paying minimums on the others. This saves thousands in interest and creates momentum.

Here's the relationship secret: tackle debt together, celebrate victories together. Make it a team sport, not a source of shame. Every extra payment is a win for your future selves.

It's not how much money you make, but how much money you keep, how hard it works for you, and how many generations you keep it for.

‚Äî Ray Dalio

üí° Action Step #4

List all your debts by interest rate. Commit to paying an extra $100-500/month toward the highest rate debt. Set a target payoff date and celebrate milestones together.
